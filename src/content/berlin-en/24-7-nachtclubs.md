Nowhere does the round-the-clock weekend quite like Berlin. A handful of the city's biggest clubs open on Friday or Saturday night and simply don't close until Monday morning, thanks to a licensing culture that lets the party run without a curfew. For visitors, it's one of the most surreal and unforgettable things about the city.

## Key takeaways

- Berlin has no mandatory closing time, so clubs can run all weekend.
- The biggest venues stay open from Friday or Saturday into Monday.
- You can arrive at almost any hour and find a full floor.
- Time your visit for the legendary Sunday sessions.
- Pace yourself - this is endurance clubbing.

## How it works

Clubs like Berghain, Sisyphos, Kater Blau and About Blank keep going all weekend, with the crowd ebbing and flowing across the days. Sunday is the connoisseur's slot: turn up in the late morning and you'll find some of the best music and deepest atmosphere of the whole weekend.

## Making it count

You don't have to stay for 30 hours to get it - even a well-timed Sunday afternoon session shows you what makes Berlin special. Just remember to eat, hydrate and take it steady.

## Plan your night

Most all-weekend clubs sit in [Friedrichshain](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-friedrichshain/) and along the Spree. Book a [hotel nearby](/en/hotels/) so bed is only a short walk away whenever you finish.
