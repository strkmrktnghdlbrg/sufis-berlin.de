Techno gets the headlines, but Berlin's electronic scene is far broader - house, minimal, electro, disco and everything in between fill the city's floors every weekend. If you love dance music but want more melody, groove or variety than a hard techno room, this is your map to the sound.

## Key takeaways

- Berlin's electronic scene reaches well beyond techno.
- House, minimal, electro and disco all have dedicated floors.
- Many clubs run multiple rooms with different sounds.
- The scene is strongest along the Spree and in the east.
- Nights start late and often run past sunrise.

## Where the sound lives

Panorama Bar, above Berghain, is the city's temple of house and disco. Watergate's history looms large over riverside electronic nights, and clubs like Sisyphos and Kater Blau weave house and melodic techno through their marathon weekends. Smaller rooms across Kreuzberg and Neukölln fill in the gaps with everything from Italo-disco to leftfield electro.

## Reading the room

Part of the fun is that a single club can shift from deep house to pounding techno as the night wears on. Check who's playing, arrive with an open mind, and let the floor take you where it's going.

## Plan your night

The core of the scene sits in [Friedrichshain](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-friedrichshain/) and [Kreuzberg](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-kreuzberg/). Base yourself at a [hotel nearby](/en/hotels/) so the walk home is short, whatever hour the last track lands.
