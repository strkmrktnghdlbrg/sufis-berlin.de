Not every Berlin night is a warehouse rave. The city has a deep, living jazz tradition - intimate basements, corner bars with a house trio, and legendary stages where world-class players drop in. If you want live music, a good cocktail and a room where you can actually hear the person next to you, this is your scene.

## Key takeaways

- Berlin has a rich jazz and blues tradition alongside its club culture.
- Venues range from tiny basements to established concert stages.
- Expect live sets, warm rooms and great drinks.
- A perfect option for an earlier, more relaxed night.
- Strong in the west, especially around Charlottenburg.

## The classic rooms

A-Trane near Savignyplatz is a Berlin institution, hosting international names in an intimate setting. Quasimodo, one of the city's oldest jazz clubs, brings blues, soul and funk to a cozy cellar. Smaller bars across the city keep the tradition alive with weekly live sessions and jam nights.

## An easier evening

Jazz nights tend to start early and end at a civilized hour, which makes them a lovely counterpoint to Berlin's late club culture - or a warm-up before you head on. Book ahead for the bigger names, as the best rooms are small.

## Plan your night

The jazz scene leans toward [Charlottenburg](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-charlottenburg/) in the elegant west. Pair a set with a [charming hotel nearby](/en/hotels/) for a refined, music-filled evening.
