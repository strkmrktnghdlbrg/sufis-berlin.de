Berlin has been a beacon for the LGBTQ+ community for over a century, and its queer nightlife is among the most vibrant and open in the world. From historic gay bars to boundary-pushing club nights, the city offers welcoming spaces for every part of the community - and visitors of every stripe are made to feel at home.

## Key takeaways

- Berlin has a long, proud history as an LGBTQ+ capital.
- Queer nightlife ranges from cozy bars to major club nights.
- Schöneberg is the historic center of the scene.
- Many mixed clubs are explicitly inclusive and welcoming.
- Pride and CSD in summer are a highlight of the calendar.

## Where the scene lives

The area around Nollendorfplatz in Schöneberg has been the heart of Berlin's gay nightlife since the 1920s, packed with bars and long-running institutions. Beyond it, some of the city's biggest clubs host legendary queer nights, and dedicated parties run across Kreuzberg and Neukölln. Whatever your scene, there's a room for it here.

## Feeling at home

Berlin's queer spaces are known for their openness, but they also value consent and respect - read the room, follow house rules, and enjoy one of the most liberated nightlife cultures anywhere.

## Plan your night

Historic [Schöneberg](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-schoeneberg/) is the natural base, with more parties across the eastern neighborhoods. Book a [hotel nearby](/en/hotels/) and dive in.
