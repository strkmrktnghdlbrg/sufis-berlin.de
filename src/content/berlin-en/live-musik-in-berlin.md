If your idea of a great night is a band in a small room rather than a DJ on a big floor, Berlin has you covered. The city is packed with music bars and intimate stages where indie, rock, folk and singer-songwriters play most nights of the week - often for the price of a drink.

## Key takeaways

- Berlin has a thriving live-music scene beyond its clubs.
- Venues range from tiny music bars to mid-size concert halls.
- Indie, rock, folk and experimental acts all have homes here.
- A great option for an earlier, band-focused night.
- Spread across Kreuzberg, Neukölln and the east.

## Where the bands play

Rooms like Lido and SO36 in Kreuzberg are Berlin institutions with decades of history. Countless smaller bars host free or low-cover live sets, and the city's mid-size halls bring in touring acts year-round. Between them, there's live music somewhere in Berlin every single night.

## Making the most of it

Check listings the day of - many of the best shows are small and word-of-mouth. Doors are usually relaxed, so you can catch a set and still move on to a club afterward if the night calls for it.

## Plan your night

The live scene centers on [Kreuzberg](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-kreuzberg/) and [Neukölln](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-neukoelln/). Book a [hotel nearby](/en/hotels/) and build your evening around the show.
