Friedrichshain is the epicenter of Berlin nightlife. If the city is the techno capital of the world, this neighborhood is its downtown - a dense belt of clubs along the Spree that draws dancers from every corner of the planet. From the world's most famous club to raw alternative spaces, this is where the legend of Berlin's nights was written.

## Key takeaways

- Friedrichshain is the epicenter of Berlin's club scene.
- Home to Berghain, Kater Blau, About Blank and more.
- Techno and electronic music dominate, with plenty of variety.
- Clubs cluster along the Spree and around the RAW-Gelände.
- The best base for a nightlife-focused trip.

## The essential clubs

Berghain, in a former power plant, is the crown jewel - hard techno below, house and disco in Panorama Bar above, and a famously selective door. Kater Blau brings a warmer, riverside energy with a festival feel, while About Blank pairs serious floors with an engaged, community-minded ethos. The RAW-Gelände adds a cluster of alternative venues and street art.

## Getting in and lasting

Keep groups small, dress understated, stay calm at the door, and don't film. Many clubs run all weekend, so pace yourself and save something for the legendary Sunday sessions.
