Prenzlauer Berg offers a gentler kind of Berlin night. Once the center of the city's post-Wall bohemia, it's now a relaxed, elegant neighborhood of wine bars, cocktail spots and cozy pubs set among some of the best-preserved pre-war streets in the city. If you'd rather sip than rave, this is your kind of evening.

## Key takeaways

- Prenzlauer Berg has a relaxed, refined nightlife.
- Wine bars, cocktail spots and cozy pubs lead the way.
- Beautiful pre-war streets set the scene.
- A calmer alternative to the eastern club districts.
- Bigger clubs are a short ride south.

## The scene

The area around Kollwitzplatz and Helmholtzplatz is full of intimate wine bars, characterful pubs and good cocktail rooms, all easy to reach on foot. It's a neighborhood built for lingering conversations and unhurried evenings rather than late-night marathons - though a nightcap can always turn into more.

## An easy base

Prenzlauer Berg borders Mitte and sits just north of Friedrichshain, so a relaxed evening here never puts the louder scenes out of reach.
