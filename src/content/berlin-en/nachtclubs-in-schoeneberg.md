Schöneberg is the historic heart of Berlin's queer nightlife. The area around Nollendorfplatz has been a hub of gay life since the 1920s, and it remains one of the warmest, most welcoming nightlife neighborhoods in the city - a place of long-running bars, community institutions and a real sense of history.

## Key takeaways

- Schöneberg is the historic center of Berlin's LGBTQ+ scene.
- Nollendorfplatz is the heart of the action.
- Expect classic bars, community spaces and welcoming clubs.
- A warm, storied neighborhood with a relaxed vibe.
- Central and well connected to the rest of the city.

## The scene

The streets around Nollendorfplatz and Motzstrasse are lined with bars that have been serving the community for decades, from cozy locals to livelier late-night spots. The mood is friendly and unpretentious, and the annual street festivals here are legendary. It's a neighborhood that welcomes everyone.

## Beyond the bars

Schöneberg also rewards a wander by day - the Winterfeldtplatz market, leafy squares and grand pre-war streets make it a lovely place to stay as well as go out.
