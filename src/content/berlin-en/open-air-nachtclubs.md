Come summer, Berlin's nightlife spills outdoors. Riverside beach bars, garden clubs, rooftops and open-air floors take over, and dancing under the sky - often as the sun comes up over the Spree - becomes one of the city's great pleasures. If you're visiting between late spring and early fall, this is the scene to chase.

## Key takeaways

- Berlin's open-air scene runs from roughly May to September.
- Riverbanks, gardens and rooftops all host outdoor parties.
- Sunrise and sunset sets are the highlight.
- Weather-dependent, so keep plans flexible.
- Strongest along the Spree and around Tempelhof.

## Where to dance outside

Sisyphos and Kater Blau blend indoor and outdoor areas for full-weekend, festival-like sessions. Riverside spots along the Spree offer beach-bar vibes and water views, while rooftop bars like Klunkerkranich in Neukölln are perfect for sunset drinks before the night ramps up. In summer, the former Tempelhof airfield hosts open-air events on a grand scale.

## Timing is everything

Open-air is all about the light - aim for a sunrise set after a long night, or an early-evening rooftop as the day cools. Bring a layer for when the temperature drops, and check the forecast before you commit.

## Plan your night

Look to [Friedrichshain](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-friedrichshain/) and [Tempelhof](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-tempelhof/) for the best of it. A [hotel nearby](/en/hotels/) keeps the walk home easy after a night under the sky.
