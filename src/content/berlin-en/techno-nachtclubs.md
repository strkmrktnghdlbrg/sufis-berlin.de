Berlin is the techno capital of the world. People travel from every continent for the sound, the spaces and the sheer intensity of a night here - clubs built inside old power plants and factories, sound systems tuned for the small hours, and a door culture unlike anywhere else. This guide walks you through the most important techno clubs in the city and what to expect from each.

## Key takeaways

- Berlin is a global center for techno and electronic music.
- Many of the best clubs occupy former industrial buildings.
- Each venue has its own sound, crowd and character.
- Door policies can be strict - preparation matters.
- Most clubs cluster along the Spree, especially in Friedrichshain.

## Berghain and Panorama Bar

Berghain is widely considered one of the best clubs on earth. Housed in a former power plant, it pairs a hard techno main floor with the house and disco of Panorama Bar upstairs. Its door is famously selective, so keep your group small, dress in understated black, stay calm and sober in line, and don't film. There are no guarantees, and that unpredictability is part of the legend.

## Tresor, Sisyphos and beyond

Tresor is the historic heart of Berlin techno, its tunnel floor a rite of passage. Sisyphos feels like a permanent festival, with outdoor areas, a lake and marathon weekends. Kater Blau brings a warmer, riverside energy. Between them they cover the full spectrum of the city's sound.

## Plan your night

Most of these clubs sit in or near [Friedrichshain](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-friedrichshain/), so it's the smart neighborhood to base yourself. Book a [hotel nearby](/en/hotels/), pace yourself, and remember that the best sets often land long after sunrise.
