Schöneberg carries a lot of history - it was David Bowie and Iggy Pop's Berlin in the 1970s, and it remains the historic heart of the city's queer nightlife. Today it's a warm, varied neighborhood of leafy squares, weekly markets and long-standing bars, and its boutique hotels reflect that layered character. Stay here for a base that feels genuinely local, with plenty of nightlife within reach.

## Key takeaways

- Schöneberg is the historic center of Berlin's LGBTQ+ scene.
- Boutique hotels here mix personal service with individual, characterful design.
- Leafy squares, markets and classic bars give the neighborhood a relaxed feel.
- A great base for travelers who want culture, history and nightlife in one place.
- Well connected to the rest of the city by U-Bahn and S-Bahn.

## A neighborhood with real character

Schöneberg rewards wandering. The Winterfeldtplatz market is one of the best in the city, the Akazienstrasse is lined with cafes and independent shops, and the quiet residential streets are full of grand pre-war buildings. Boutique hotels here tend to be intimate and personal, run by people who know the area and can point you to the good stuff.

## Nightlife and the queer scene

The stretch around Nollendorfplatz has been the center of Berlin's gay nightlife for a century, and it's still going strong. Whether you're here for that scene specifically or just want lively, welcoming bars nearby, our guide to [LGBTQ+ nightlife](/en/berlin/lgbt-freundliche-nachtclubs/) and the wider [Schöneberg scene](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-schoeneberg/) will steer you right.

## Easy to explore from

Schöneberg sits just south of the city's center, so you're never far from the main sights or the bigger club districts. Strong transit links make it simple to spend your day in Mitte and your night wherever the music takes you.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Schöneberg good for LGBTQ+ travelers?

Very. It's the historic heart of Berlin's queer nightlife, with welcoming bars and clubs around Nollendorfplatz.

### What are the hotels like here?

Mostly small, personal boutique hotels with individual design and a local, friendly feel.

### Is Schöneberg central?

It's just south of the center and extremely well connected, so the main sights and nightlife districts are close.

### Is there a market worth visiting?

Yes - the Saturday market on Winterfeldtplatz is one of the city's best for food and local produce.
