Charlottenburg is the elegant face of West Berlin. This is the neighborhood of the Kurfürstendamm - the city's grand shopping boulevard - of tree-lined Savignyplatz, and of a more grown-up, refined kind of night out. Its charming hotels range from historic grande dames to smaller design-led stays, and they suit travelers who want polish, comfort and a quieter base with excellent food and jazz close at hand.

## Key takeaways

- Charlottenburg is the elegant, classic side of West Berlin.
- Hotels range from historic and refined to youthful boutique stays.
- The Ku'damm and Savignyplatz anchor top shopping and dining.
- A calmer, more grown-up base - great for jazz and cocktails.
- Superb transit links to the rest of the city.

## Grand boulevards and leafy squares

Charlottenburg feels a world away from the grit of the eastern neighborhoods, and that's the point. Stroll the Ku'damm for flagship stores and cafes, settle in around Savignyplatz for the restaurants tucked under the S-Bahn arches, or visit Charlottenburg Palace and its gardens. The hotels here match the setting: gracious, comfortable and often full of character.

## A more refined night out

Nightlife in Charlottenburg skews classic - think jazz bars, cocktail lounges and long dinners rather than warehouse raves. If that's your speed, our guides to [jazz & blues](/en/berlin/jazz-und-blues-nachtclubs/) and [nightlife in Charlottenburg](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-charlottenburg/) point you to the best rooms in the area. And when you want the bigger clubs, the U-Bahn gets you east in no time.

## Comfortable and well connected

Because Charlottenburg is quieter, it makes a restful base while still keeping the whole city within easy reach. Frequent S-Bahn and U-Bahn service means the eastern nightlife districts are a straightforward ride away when the night calls for it.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Charlottenburg a good area to stay in?

Yes, especially if you want an elegant, calmer base with great shopping, dining and comfortable hotels.

### What's the nightlife like?

More refined than the east - jazz bars, cocktail lounges and good restaurants, with the bigger clubs a short ride away.

### Is it good for shopping?

It's the best in the city. The Ku'damm and its side streets are Berlin's classic shopping district.

### How far is it from the club districts?

A comfortable U-Bahn or S-Bahn ride east reaches Friedrichshain, Kreuzberg and the rest.
