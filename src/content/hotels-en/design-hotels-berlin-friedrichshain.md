If you came to Berlin for the clubs, Friedrichshain is your neighborhood. This is the techno heartland - home to Berghain and a whole belt of venues along the Spree - and the design hotels here are built for people who plan their trip around the dance floor. Modern, stylish and close to the action, they let you sleep late, roll out at night and walk home when the sun comes up.

## Key takeaways

- Friedrichshain is the epicenter of Berlin's techno and club scene.
- Design hotels here favor a modern, contemporary look with a young energy.
- You're walking distance from major clubs, live venues and riverside bars.
- Ideal for travelers whose trip revolves around music and nightlife.
- Strong transit links make the rest of the city easy to reach.

## Built for the night

Few neighborhoods put you closer to Berlin's legendary nights. Friedrichshain's design hotels tend to be sleek and unfussy, with the kind of layout that works when your schedule runs on club time. Late checkout, a solid breakfast whenever you surface, and a location that means the walk to the floor - and back - is short. Our guide to [nightlife in Friedrichshain](/en/berlin/nachtclubs-in-friedrichshain/) maps out the venues nearby.

## More than clubs

There's plenty to do before dark, too. RAW-Gelände is a hub of street art, bars and markets; Boxhagener Platz anchors a grid of cafes, vintage shops and Sunday flea markets; and the East Side Gallery - the longest surviving stretch of the Berlin Wall - runs along the river. It's an easygoing neighborhood by day and a serious one by night.

## Getting around

Friedrichshain is well served by S-Bahn, U-Bahn and trams, so the museums of Mitte or a day trip further afield are simple to organize. And with the Oberbaum Bridge right here, Kreuzberg's bars are just across the water.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Friedrichshain the best area for clubbing?

For techno and electronic music, yes - it's as central to the scene as it gets, with major clubs within walking distance.

### What are the hotels like?

Mostly modern, design-forward stays with a contemporary feel that fits the neighborhood's young, creative crowd.

### Can I still enjoy Friedrichshain if I'm not clubbing?

Absolutely. Street art, riverside walks, markets and cafes make it a great neighborhood around the clock.

### How far is it to the rest of Berlin?

Very close. Excellent transit connections put Mitte and the main sights within easy reach.
