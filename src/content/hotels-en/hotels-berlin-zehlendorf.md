Zehlendorf is Berlin's leafy, upscale southwest - a district of grand villas, lakes and the vast Grunewald forest. This is where the city comes to breathe: swim at Wannsee, walk on Pfaueninsel among peacocks, or visit the museums of Dahlem. The hotels here are refined and restful, suiting travelers who want quiet, greenery and comfort, with the center reachable by a straightforward S-Bahn ride.

## Key takeaways

- Zehlendorf is the green, upscale southwest of Berlin.
- Hotels here are refined, calm and comfortable.
- Lakes, forest and cultural sites define the district.
- Best for travelers who want tranquility over nightlife.
- Connected to the center by S-Bahn.

## Lakes, forest and grand villas

Zehlendorf feels almost rural in places. The Grunewald forest and the Havel lakes dominate, Wannsee draws swimmers and sailors in summer, and Pfaueninsel - a UNESCO-listed island park - is one of the loveliest spots in the city. The hotels match the setting: gracious, quiet and comfortable, a proper retreat from the urban rush.

## The city when you want it

This is not a nightlife district - it's the opposite, and deliberately so. When you're ready for a night out, the S-Bahn runs into the center and on toward the eastern club districts. Our [genre guides](/en/genres/) help you plan where to head once you're in town.

## Refined and restful

Zehlendorf is for travelers who value calm, space and greenery, and don't mind a short ride to the action. With reliable transit and a peaceful setting, it's a refined base for a gentler kind of Berlin trip.

## Frequently asked questions

### What's Zehlendorf like?

Green, quiet and upscale, with lakes, forest and grand villas in the city's southwest.

### What are the hotels like?

Refined, calm and comfortable - a restful retreat rather than a party base.

### Is there nightlife here?

No - it's a peaceful district. The clubs are a ride away in the center and east.

### Is it good for nature?

Excellent. The Grunewald forest, Wannsee and Pfaueninsel are all here.
